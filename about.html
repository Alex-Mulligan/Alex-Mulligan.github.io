Who am I Anyway?

Hello There!

My name is Alex Mulligan. I am an Undegraduate Student Researcher at the University of California, Irvine studying machine learning and artificial intelligence. Currently, I am researching a method to use convolutional autoencoder networks and deep learning to perform anomaly detection on astronomical sky survey data with the goal of identifying gravitational lenses. My passions include computer science, machine learning, astronomy, and trail running.

In my research, I have had the priviledge of working in tandem with the Gaia GraL.

When I am not researching and developing new methodologies for classifying quasars, I am either tinkering with small projects of my own, on the trail running, playing some games, or hanging out with friends.
